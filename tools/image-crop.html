How to Use Our Image Crop Tool

  1. Upload your image by clicking the "Upload Your Image" area or dragging and dropping your file. We support JPG, PNG, BMP, GIF, TIFF, and WebP formats.
  2. Select an aspect ratio from the dropdown menu or use freeform cropping for complete control.
  3. Adjust the crop area by dragging the corners or edges of the crop box. You can reposition the entire box by dragging from the center.
  4. Click the "Crop Image" button to process your image. The cropping happens instantly in your browser.
  5. Compare the results using the side-by-side preview.
  6. Download your cropped image by clicking the "Download Cropped Image" button.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will cropping reduce my image quality?

No, cropping doesn't reduce image quality. The cropped portion maintains the original quality of your image. However, if you enlarge a small cropped area, it may appear pixelated.

Is there a file size limit?

We recommend images under 20MB for optimal performance. Larger files may take longer to process.

Are my images secure?

Absolutely! All image processing happens directly in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to our servers, ensuring complete privacy and security.

What image formats do you support?

We support JPG, PNG, BMP, GIF, TIFF, and WebP formats for both input and output.

Can I crop multiple images at once?

Currently, our crop tool processes one image at a time. For batch cropping, you'll need to crop each image separately.

What's the best aspect ratio for social media?

It depends on the platform. Instagram posts typically use 1:1 (square), Instagram stories use 9:16, Facebook posts use various ratios, and Twitter works well with 16:9.
